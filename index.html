Android rooting basics.

General information — always get a compatibility check before deciding.

GUIDE

What is Magisk?

Magisk is a systemless rooting method that modifies your device without touching the system partition, which is why it's the most common root tool today.

GUIDE

How custom ROMs work

A custom ROM replaces your phone's stock Android software with a community-built alternative, often for extra features or longer update support.

GUIDE

Rooting: benefits & risks

Rooting unlocks deep customization and ad-blocking at the system level, but it can void your warranty and break apps that check for root.

faq

Frequently asked questions.

Is rooting safe? +
When done correctly on a supported device, yes — but there's always some risk. That's why a free compatibility check and backup come first on every job.
Will I lose my warranty? +
Usually, yes. Rooting and bootloader unlocking typically void the manufacturer's warranty. This is disclosed before any work begins.
Can I unroot my device? +
In most cases yes, though the process depends on your device and how it was rooted. Ask during your compatibility check.
Will banking apps still work? +
Some banking and payment apps detect root and may stop working or need extra hiding setup. This is discussed upfront so there are no surprises.
How long does it take? +
It depends on the device and service — ask for a time estimate during your compatibility check.
What phones do you support? +
Samsung, Xiaomi, Redmi, POCO, Pixel, OnePlus, Nothing, ASUS, Motorola, Sony, and selected Oppo/vivo/Realme models. Send your exact model to confirm.
